Instructions

Systematic reviews are a crucial component of evidence-based research, providing a comprehensive and rigorous synthesis of existing literature. By aggregating and critically appraising multiple studies, systematic reviews enable researchers to draw more robust conclusions, identify research gaps, and inform decision-making processes. 

We are a team of researchers at the University of Zurich, Switzerland, and would want to hear your thoughts and opinions regarding systematic reviews. The survey will take approximately 10-15 minutes, and there are no right or wrong answers. This study should pose no potential discomfort or risks to you if you choose to participate. Your responses will be kept strictly anonymous and confidential. In case you have any questions, please do not hesitate to contact the investigator (Dr Marianna Rosso, marianna.rosso@uzh.ch).

Please read the following text carefully and check the box if you agree:    

- You can terminate your participation at any time, without giving a reason for it. 

- Your data will be treated anonymously. However, it will be impossible to remove your data at a later stage. (If you have any concerns, simply terminate your participation.) 

- There are no foreseeable negative consequences to taking part in this study. 

-  The results of this study may be used in scientific publications, so we kindly request that you take this study at a time when you are able to pay attention to the instructions and take your time. 

Your efforts are greatly appreciated and will help us improve systematic reviews! As a token of our gratitude, upon completing the survey, you'll have the opportunity to be drafted for a complimentary consultation with our group for your upcoming systematic review. Your contact information will be handled separately, and the decision to provide it will be entirely yours.
